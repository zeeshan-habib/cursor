## The Actual Gaps to Solve

The parents are largely self-sufficient. What remains:

1. **Caregiver cost** — when they need a live-in helper, pension may not fully cover it
   - Caregiver: ₹12,000–20,000/month added to ₹20K baseline = potential ₹10K–₹18K shortfall/month
2. **Transport** — not every issue qualifies for military hospital; specialist visits, dental, eye, routine care in Salipur/Bhubaneswar come out of pocket
3. **Quality-of-life** — AC, appliances, phone/device upgrades for video calls, occasional travel to Hyderabad
4. **Emergency buffer** — one-time large expenses (surgery outside military system, specialist in metro, emergencies)

## The Care System: 4 Layers

### 1. Daily Care Layer
- Starts: part-time help (cooking, errands, medication reminders)
- Scales to: live-in caregiver/nurse as needed
- Cost: ₹12,000–25,000/month when activated
- Funded by: pension first, brother supplements, Zee backstops

### 2. Healthcare Access Layer
- Military hospital for major admissions — already in place
- Establish a standing relationship with a clinic/doctor in Salipur now (not during a crisis)
- Arrange a reliable transport option (trusted driver/auto, small monthly retainer)
- Telemedicine for non-emergency specialist access: Practo, Apollo 24|7, mfine (~₹500–800/consult)

### 3. Emergency Layer
- Dedicated cash buffer for hospitalizations outside military system, unexpected travel, surgeries
- Target: ₹3–5 lakh (≈ $6,000–8,000 CAD) liquid, not invested
- Zee builds this via dedicated HISA in Canada (see funding plan below)

### 4. Coordination Layer
- Brother = primary on-ground contact
- Explicit agreement needed: who calls whom, who visits, decision-making during a health event
- Consider power of attorney discussion (early, not during crisis)
