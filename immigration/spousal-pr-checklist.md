## Phase 3 — Relationship evidence (critical)

IRCC must be satisfied the marriage is genuine — not for immigration purposes. Organize by category:

| # | Evidence type | Examples | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3.1 | Proof of contact over time | WhatsApp/chat exports, call logs, emails — dated, spanning relationship | Both |
| 3.2 | Visits & travel | Boarding passes, visa stamps, hotel bookings, photos from visits | Both |
| 3.3 | Photos together | Dated, captioned — wedding, family events, daily life | Both |
| 3.4 | Financial ties | Transfer records, remittance receipts, gifts, shared expenses | Both |
| 3.5 | Family involvement | Photos with families, wedding cards, affidavits from parents/siblings | Both |
| 3.6 | Wedding documentation | Invitation, ceremony photos, religious/civil registration | Both |
| 3.7 | Statutory declarations | Letters from friends/family who know you as a couple | Both |
| 3.8 | Letter of explanation | Narrative timeline: how met, relationship milestones, why genuine | Zee + wife |

**Outland note:** Long-distance couples often have less joint lease/bank account evidence — compensate with communication logs, visit history, and affidavits.

## Common refusal triggers to avoid

- Inconsistent dates across forms vs documents
- Weak or generic relationship evidence
- Missing translations
- Outdated form versions
- Unexplained gaps in employment or travel history
- Incomplete police certificates
